# The Book Lifecycle

Huerta Group Publishing · Author Operating System
Status: adopted July 2026. The lifecycle is one of the platform's
organizing principles (Product Constitution XIV): each stage preserves a
different kind of work, and future capabilities appear as the manuscript
matures. Status remains a stated fact on the Book Record — edited like a
name, never gated, never a workflow engine.

The future-capability alignments below are **documentation only**. None
of them exist; none are promised on any surface of the product. They
record where each future capability will naturally live so that no
capability is ever designed without knowing its stage.

---

## 1. Discovery — *"What am I trying to say?"*

The book is still being discovered. Core ideas, philosophy,
conversations, Author Memory, Book Constitution, Master Outline, Concept
Dictionary, research, and overall direction are actively evolving. Very
little manuscript prose may exist — and that is the point of the stage.

*Future capabilities that belong here:* Book Constitution, Master
Outline, Concept Dictionary (built — Capability 2), Discovery Log,
Conversation Import, Research Vault.

## 2. Writing — *"How do I say it?"*

The manuscript is actively being written. The foundational documents
continue evolving while chapters are created; the memory system and the
manuscript grow together.

*Future capabilities:* Chapter Workspace, AI Draft Assistance (grounded
in the Book Assembled Memory, never a substitute for it), Cross
References, Concept References.

## 3. Editorial Review — *"Does this accomplish its purpose?"*

The manuscript is complete enough for systematic review against the
book's own constitution and its author's memory.

*Future capabilities:* Constitution Compliance, Voice Consistency,
Redundancy Detection, Timeline Review, Concept Consistency, Authenticity
Review.

## 4. Revision — *"How can it become better?"*

Editorial findings are being incorporated. The append-only version
machinery built in Capabilities 1–2 is the foundation everything in this
stage stands on.

*Future capabilities:* Editorial Notes, Change Comparison, Revision
Queue, Version Diff.

## 5. Final Manuscript — *"Is this the book I intended to write?"*

The manuscript is considered editorially complete. Only publication
preparation remains.

*Future capabilities:* Manuscript Lock, Publication Preview, Final
Proofreading.

## 6. Ready for Publication — *"Is it ready for readers?"*

Publishing assets are completed: interior formatting, cover, metadata,
ISBN, distribution setup.

*Future capabilities:* EPUB, PDF, Cover Assets, Metadata, ISBN,
Publishing Checklist.

## 7. Published — *"How does it live in the world?"*

The work has been released.

*Future capabilities:* Distribution, Edition History, Future Revision
Planning.

## 8. Archived — *"What should history preserve?"*

The project is no longer active. The permanent record remains preserved
— archiving is a stated fact, never a deletion.

*Future capabilities:* Permanent Preservation.

---

## Design consequences

- **Progressive revelation.** The interface should reveal the tools
  appropriate to the book's current stage rather than presenting every
  capability at once (Product Constitution XIV). A book in Discovery
  should feel like a study; a book in Revision should feel like an
  editor's desk. This governs *presentation and emphasis* — it never
  locks an author out of their own record.
- **Stages are self-declared.** The author or publisher states where the
  book stands; the platform never decides for them, requires approvals,
  or enforces transitions. Any stage can follow any stage.
- **Each new capability must name its stage** before it is built, in its
  blueprint — the stage question is part of the capability's design
  brief.
